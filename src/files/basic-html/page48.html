However,  this  ﬁctitious  story  actually  represents 
the  challenges  faced  in  international  trades 
particularly by SMEs therefore causing manytrades 
to be lost. Due to lack of trust and transparency, 
Banks have had to introduce stringent veriﬁcation

causing huge gap between requirements for trade 
ﬁnance and support provided by the banks.  
In  a  recent  report  by  the  African  Development 
Bank, African banks provide a total of $430 billion in 
trade  ﬁnance,  which  accounts  for  one-third  total 
trade  in  Africa.  According  to  the  report  only  28 
percent  of  banks  total  trade  ﬁnance  portfolio 
beneﬁts SMEs; this relatively low share of SMEs was 
attributed  to  high-risk  perception  of  this  client 
category. Is there an alternative solution that the 
banks can adopted to reduce those risks? While 
blockchain is being used across  multiple use cases 
ranging  from  manufacturing,  healthcare  to  real 
estate or government, one sectorbeneﬁting from 
this legendary  technology is trade ﬁnance.
 Although, one of the main arguments touted by 
blockchain /cryptocurrency enthusiastsis the idea 
of  disrupting  the  banking  sector  which  is  often 
viewed  as  a  complete  exodus  of  the  traditional 
banking  system,  but  lies  the  possibility  of  this 
current status quo leveraging on the technology to 
provide efﬁcient ﬁnancialservices to all sundry. 
Blockchain  capabilities  can  deliver  banking 
innovations to provide a delightful experience for 
customers by reducing transaction timerates to its 
barest minimum. The technology eliminates manual 
processes,  unnecessary  intermediaries  in  trade 
ﬁnance,  digital  identities  and  cross-border 
payments.  With  blockchain,  SMEs  can  conduct 
business  through  banksrapidly  and  securely, 
migrating from paper based to blockchain-secured 
transaction  records,  which  can  enable  easier 
expansion to undeserved markets. This is a perfect 
combinationof old norm and the new. 
Deploying  blockchain  technology  bring  a  lot  of 
beneﬁts  to  trade  ﬁnance.  These  beneﬁts  can  be 
categorised as efﬁciency, traceability, auditability, 
transparency and security.
Efﬁciency – Blockchain allows two parties to directly 
transact  without  an  intermediary  involved.  Using 
smart contracts, the parties involved can have their 
terms of contract authenticated on it. This helps to 
minimize  transactioncharges.  Blockchain 
application  in  trade  ﬁnance  wouldreduce 
transaction time to few hours compared to the 5-7 
business  working  days  operating  in  the  current 
traditional system.
Traceability – Blockchain technology allows buyers 
and issuing banks track goods and digital assetsas it 
transits  through  the  supply  chain  process.  This 
allows  new  ﬁnancing  opportunities  and  further 
imposemore  conﬁdence  inbanks  to  ﬁnancefuture 
trades.
