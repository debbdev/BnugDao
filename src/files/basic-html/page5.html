s technology continues to evolve, new 
investment opportunities are cropping 
up every now and then. Sometimes 
referred to as disruptive technologies, 
emerging technologies can be deﬁned 
as sections of the tech sector that 
are set to have a massive impact on human lives in the 
next decade.
Many types of technology fall under that umbrella, but 
a few of the best-known categories are artiﬁcial 
intelligence (AI), Blockchain, Virtual Reality (VR), robotics 
and mobile technology.
What is it?
Research ﬁrms like PwC and Gartner are 
tracking as many as 150 different 
technologies with the aim of identifying 
the tech sectors that are \showing 
promise\ and could be impactful in the 
next few years.
Back in 2016, PwC identiﬁed eight 
emerging technologies that are set to 
converge in the virtual and the physical 
worlds. Dubbed the \essential 
eight,\ these new 
technology
areas are AI, 
robots, drones, 
virtual reality, 
augmented 
reality, 
blockchain, 3D 
printing
and the Internet 
of Things.
Experts agree 
that emerging 
technology 
encompasses 
diverse areas 
of the market, 
although
standouts are coming to the fore.
(1) Future prospects
As mentioned, AI and Blockchain are a key part of the 
emerging technology space, and some of the biggest 
growth is forecast in this arena. In fact, the market is 
predicted to reach a market value of US$733.7 billion 
by 2027, growing at a compound annual growth rate of 
42.2 percent between 2020 and 2027. The current $3 
Trillion Dollar cryptocurrency market is a strong 
indicator of what is to be expected in the coming years. 
Guys,...just HODL your bags!
(2) Stocks
For investors looking to enter the emerging technology 
space, stocks are the obvious choice. And considering 
that emerging technologies involve multiple sections 
of the tech sector, there’s certainly no shortage of 
companies for investors to look at.
(3) ETFs
For investors who want to put money into the 
emerging tech space as a whole rather than a 
speciﬁc company involved in the sector, exchange-
traded funds (ETFs) are a popular way to go. They 
provide exposure to a basket of products, 
such as stocks, and reduce risk by 
ensuring that investors are not 
overexposed to any one asset.
